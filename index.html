📚 Learn About Financial Calculators

🏠 What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed payment you make monthly to repay a loan. It consists of principal and interest components. Our EMI calculator uses the standard amortization formula used by all Indian banks including SBI, HDFC, ICICI. Works for home loans, car loans, personal loans, and education loans.

📈 SIP & Compounding

Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) lets you invest a fixed amount in mutual funds every month. The power of compounding means your returns generate their own returns. Starting a ₹5,000 SIP at 12% for 30 years can grow to ₹1.76 Crore — with just ₹18L invested!

📋 Tax Regime Comparison

Budget 2025 made the New Regime default with enhanced slabs. No tax up to ₹12 lakh income (with standard deduction). Old Regime still benefits those with high deductions under 80C, 80D, HRA. Our calculator compares both instantly.

🏦 FD vs RD: Which is Better?

Fixed Deposits (FD) are lump-sum investments at guaranteed interest rates. Recurring Deposits (RD) let you invest monthly. FDs offer slightly higher rates due to larger principal. Both are safe, DICGC-insured up to ₹5 Lakh per depositor per bank.

🧾 GST in India

GST has 4 main slabs: 5%, 12%, 18%, 28%. For intra-state supply, GST splits equally as CGST + SGST. For inter-state supply, only IGST is charged. Most services are at 18% GST. Our calculator handles both add-GST and remove-GST scenarios.

🏖️ Retirement Planning

With Indian inflation at 5-7%, your expenses double every 10-12 years. A ₹40,000/month expense today becomes ₹2.3L/month in 30 years at 6% inflation. Starting SIP early with equity exposure is the most effective way to build a retirement corpus.

🎯 CIBIL Score — Why It Matters

Your CIBIL score (300–900) directly impacts your loan interest rate. A score of 750+ gets you the best home loan rates (~8.25%), while a score below 650 can push your rate to 10–13%+. On a ₹50 lakh loan, this difference costs you ₹8,000+/month more! Check your score and get personalised tips using our CIBIL Score Checker above.

🏠 Rent vs Buy — The Right Decision

There's no universal answer — it depends on property appreciation in your city, your loan rate, rental yield, and how long you plan to stay. Our Rent vs Buy calculator compares the total cost of both options over your chosen period, factoring in opportunity cost of the down payment and rental escalation.

📊 Loan Eligibility — How Banks Decide

Banks use FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) — typically 50–60% of net monthly income. After deducting existing EMIs, the remaining amount sets your max new EMI. Factors that boost eligibility: high income, no existing EMIs, co-applicant, longer tenure, and a CIBIL score above 750.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How is EMI calculated?▼
EMI uses the formula: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1), where P = loan principal, r = monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), n = total months. For example, a ₹25 lakh home loan at 8.5% for 20 years has an EMI of approximately ₹21,700. The higher your tenure, the lower the EMI but more total interest paid.
Which is better — New or Old Tax Regime?▼
If your total deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, home loan interest) exceed approximately ₹3.75 lakh, Old Regime is usually better. For salaried individuals with limited investments, New Regime with its lower slab rates and ₹75,000 standard deduction is more beneficial. Our calculator compares both side by side to help you decide.
How much should I invest in SIP for ₹1 Crore?▼
At 12% expected returns: ₹5,000/month for 25 years = ~₹95 Lakhs, ₹10,000/month for 20 years = ~₹99 Lakhs, ₹15,000/month for 17 years = ~₹1.03 Crore. The key is starting early to leverage the power of compounding. Even small amounts grow significantly over long periods.
Is FD interest taxable in India?▼
Yes, FD interest is fully taxable and added to your income. Banks deduct TDS at 10% if annual interest exceeds ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens). If your total income is below the taxable limit, you can submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS deduction.
How is loan eligibility determined?▼
Banks use FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) — typically 50-60% of net monthly income. After deducting existing EMIs, the remaining amount determines your maximum new EMI. From that, the maximum loan is reverse-calculated using the EMI formula. A higher income, lower existing obligations, and longer tenure increase eligibility.
Should I rent or buy a house?▼
The decision depends on factors like: property appreciation rate in your area, home loan interest rate, your rent amount vs. EMI, how long you plan to stay, and opportunity cost of the down payment. Our calculator compares the total cost of renting (with annual increases) vs. buying (EMI + opportunity cost) over your chosen time period.
What is a good CIBIL score for a home loan?▼
A CIBIL score of 750 or above is considered excellent and gets you the best home loan rates (around 8.25%–8.50%). Most banks approve home loans at 700+, though rates are slightly higher. Below 650, getting a home loan becomes difficult and expensive. Our CIBIL Score Checker shows your exact rating, interest rate impact, and personalised tips to improve.
How long does it take to improve CIBIL score?▼
With consistent positive actions (timely payments every month, reducing credit card utilisation to under 30%, no new loan applications), you can see improvement in 3–6 months. Moving from 650 to 750 typically takes 12–18 months of disciplined credit behaviour. The most impactful actions: never miss an EMI, keep credit card balances low, and don't close old accounts.
